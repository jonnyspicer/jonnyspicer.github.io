--- layout: mendokusai title: Things That Go Bump In The Night date: '2018-02-14T14:45:00.001-08:00' author: Jonny Spicer tags: modified_time: '2018-02-14T14:45:36.488-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4848445585442664375.post-4999971132667369597 blogger_orig_url: http://jonnyspicer.blogspot.com/2018/02/things-that-go-bump-in-night.html --- I very rarely watch films but after enjoying my 4-movie binge yesterday, I'm going to try and watch a few more films this week. 

I've always been a giant pussy when it comes to horror movies and games. I lasted less than 30 minutes into my namesake, Amnesia I, before alt+f4ing and never playing again. I think the last horror movie I watched was The Shining, which I survived only because of how dated it is. I have never understood the appeal of the genre - fear is a negative emotion, so why would anyone willing experience it? 

But it is nonetheless an emotion, and an experience, often an intense one. And often, simply feeling something strongly is a good reminder that we are alive and capable of feeling. After having watched a good 20 horror trailers today, I am going to try to ease myself into the genre and try and grow some testicles. Being the wannabe hipster I am, I'm going to start with some weird foreign films... on the docket tomorrow is recent Belgian film Raw and Guillermo Del Torro's Pan's Labyrinth. I'll probably write reviews of them assuming I don't shit my pants and quit halfway through. I'm hoping to build up to It and some point soon, but not gonna hold out too much hope.
